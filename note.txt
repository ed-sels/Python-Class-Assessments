Durbar is a big meeting or gathering of people in a community, often held during special events or festivals. 
It is led by the chief and elders, and there is usually drumming, dancing, and speeches.

Examples of durbars

Akwasidae Durbar is held by the Ashantis to honor their ancestors.
Hogbetsotso Durbar is celebrated by the Anlo Ewes during their festival.
Odwira Durbar is held by the Akuapem people to purify the land.

Durbars help people show their culture and come together as one community.
